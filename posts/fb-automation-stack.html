The Exact Stack I Use to Automate F&B Client Onboarding (No Code, All Results)

How I built a WhatsApp-first automation system for F&B businesses in Malaysia that handles onboarding, follow-ups, and reporting without a single line of code.

Contents

The average F&B owner in Malaysia is running customer management on three WhatsApp groups, a paper notepad, and memory. That's not an exaggeration — I've seen it firsthand. When a new customer messages in, the process is: reply manually, forget to follow up, lose them to a competitor who responded faster.

There's no CRM. No system. Just chaos that looks organised until it isn't.

I built an automation stack that fixes this — no custom code, no expensive software. Just four tools wired together correctly.

💡
The Stack WhatsApp Business API → n8n → Google Sheets → OpenClaw. Each tool does one thing well. Together they replace a full-time follow-up person.

The Stack

WhatsApp Business API The entry point. Every customer message arrives as a structured webhook. Not the WhatsApp Business app — the actual API. There's a difference, and it matters.
n8n (self-hosted) The brain. Every trigger, conditional, and transformation runs here. I run it on a RM35/month VPS. Handles hundreds of workflows without breaking a sweat.
Google Sheets The database. Not because it's ideal — because F&B staff already use it. No onboarding required. Every team member knows how to open a spreadsheet.
OpenClaw The AI layer. Classifies customer intent, handles intelligent follow-ups, and drafts responses that sound human — not like a bot.

The Workflow

A new customer sends a WhatsApp message. Here's what happens in the next 30 seconds — with no one touching a keyboard:

1

Webhook Trigger

n8n listens for incoming WhatsApp messages from the Meta API. The moment a message arrives, the workflow fires.

2

Intent Classification

An OpenClaw agent reads the message and categorises it: new inquiry, returning customer, complaint, or general question. This classification drives every downstream action.

3

Google Sheets Write

Customer phone, name, message, timestamp, category, and status are appended to the sheet. It becomes a live CRM dashboard your team can read on mobile.

4

Automated Onboarding Message

If classified as "new inquiry," n8n sends a Meta-approved WhatsApp template — personalised with the customer's name and your restaurant's details — within seconds.

5

Follow-Up Scheduling

A Wait node holds for 3 days. If the customer hasn't converted, n8n sends a second WhatsApp template — a gentle nudge with a specific offer.

6

Escalation to Human

Still no conversion? The row is flagged in the Sheet and the owner gets an internal WhatsApp alert. Human attention only arrives after automation has done everything it can.

Why This Beats a CRM

I've recommended proper CRMs to F&B clients. HubSpot, Zoho, Freshsales. Every time: the team uses it for two weeks, then goes back to WhatsApp because it's easier. The problem isn't discipline — CRMs are built for salespeople at desks, not staff on their feet in kitchens.

  • Speed. WhatsApp Business API has a 98% open rate. Your follow-up messages get read. Email open rates for the same demographic hover around 20%.
  • Cost. n8n self-hosted: RM35/month. Google Sheets: free. WhatsApp API: under RM0.30 per conversation. Total for a restaurant handling 200 inquiries: under RM100/month.
  • Familiarity. Your team already lives in WhatsApp. Your customers already live in WhatsApp. Friction: zero.
